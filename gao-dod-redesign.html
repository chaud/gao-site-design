Department of Defense

The Department of Defense (DOD) is the largest federal agency, responsible for providing the military forces needed to deter war and protect the security of the United States. GAO conducts extensive oversight of DOD's operations, programs, and spending to help ensure accountability in national defense.

GAO's Role in Defense Oversight

GAO provides Congress with independent, nonpartisan analysis of DOD programs and operations. Our work spans weapon systems acquisition, military readiness, cybersecurity, financial management, healthcare, and personnel issues. We identify opportunities to improve efficiency, reduce costs, and strengthen national security.

DOD has the most recommendations of any federal agency on GAO's High Risk List, reflecting the scope and complexity of defense operations. Our recommendations have helped save taxpayers billions of dollars and improve the effectiveness of defense programs.

Key Challenges Facing DOD

  • Financial Management: DOD remains unable to pass a full financial audit, with significant weaknesses in accounting systems and internal controls.
  • Weapon Systems Acquisition: Major programs continue to experience cost growth and schedule delays, with the F-35 program alone costing over $400 billion.
  • Cybersecurity: Critical infrastructure and weapon systems face increasing cyber threats from nation-state actors.
  • Readiness: Equipment maintenance backlogs and personnel shortages affect military readiness across all services.
